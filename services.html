Out-Patient Department

An OPD is a department in a hospital or clinic where patients receive medical consultations, treatments, and follow-up care without being admitted as inpatients. OPDs are usually staffed by general practitioners or specialists who diagnose and treat a wide range of medical conditions.

Ambulance Care

Ambulance care refers to the medical services provided by ambulance personnel during transportation of sick or injured patients from one location to another. Ambulance care can include emergency medical treatments, patient stabilization, and transportation to hospitals or other medical facilities.

Hospitals

Hospitals are healthcare facilities that provide medical treatment and care to patients who require hospitalization for illness, injury, or surgery. Hospitals offer a range of medical services, including diagnostic tests, surgeries, and specialized treatments for various medical conditions.

Nursing

Nursing refers to the profession of providing care for sick, injured, or disabled patients. Nurses work in a variety of healthcare settings, including hospitals, clinics, and long-term care facilities. They play an important role in patient care, administering medications, monitoring vital signs, and providing emotional support.

Clinic / Lab-Test

A clinic is a medical facility where patients receive outpatient care and treatment for a range of medical conditions. Clinics may offer specialized medical services such as laboratory tests, imaging services, and diagnostic procedures.

Telehealth

Telehealth refers to the delivery of healthcare services using electronic communication technologies, such as videoconferencing and remote monitoring. Telehealth services can include virtual consultations with healthcare providers, remote monitoring of patients, and online health education.

Out-Patient Clinic

Electro-Cardiogram

An electrocardiogram (ECG) is a diagnostic test that measures the electrical activity of the heart. An ECG can help diagnose various heart conditions, including arrhythmias and heart attacks.

Oxygen
