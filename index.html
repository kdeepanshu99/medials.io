Out-patient Department

An OPD is a department in a hospital or clinic where patients receive medical consultations, treatments, and follow-up care without being admitted as inpatients. OPDs are usually staffed by general practitioners or specialists who diagnose and treat a wide range of medical conditions.

Nursing

Nursing refers to the profession of providing care for sick, injured, or disabled patients. Nurses work in a variety of healthcare settings, including hospitals, clinics, and long-term care facilities. They play an important role in patient care, administering medications, monitoring vital signs, and providing emotional support.

Read more

Out-Patient Clinic

An out-patient clinic is a healthcare facility where patients receive medical treatment and care without being admitted as inpatients. Outpatient clinics may offer a range of medical services, including diagnostic tests, treatments, and follow-up care for various medical conditions.

Read more

Ambulance Care

Ambulance care refers to the medical services provided by ambulance personnel during transportation of sick or injured patients from one location to another. Ambulance care can include emergency medical treatments, patient stabilization, and transportation to hospitals or other medical facilities.

Read more

Clinic / Lab-Test

A clinic or laboratory test is a medical examination that is performed to diagnose or monitor a health condition. These tests are typically ordered by a healthcare provider and may be done in a clinic, hospital, or laboratory setting. These tests are an important tool for healthcare providers in diagnosing and monitoring health conditions.

Read more

Electro-Cardiogram

An electrocardiogram (ECG) is a diagnostic test that measures the electrical activity of the heart. An ECG can help diagnose various heart conditions, including arrhythmias and heart attacks. During an ECG, small electrodes are placed on the patient's chest, arms, and legs.

Read more

Hospitals

Hospitals are healthcare facilities that provide medical treatment and care to patients who require hospitalization for illness, injury, or surgery. Hospitals offer a range of medical services, including diagnostic tests, surgeries, medical tests and specialized treatments for various medical conditions.

Read more

Telehealth

Telehealth refers to the delivery of healthcare services using electronic communication technologies. Telehealth services can include virtual consultations with healthcare providers,doctors, and online health education. It can also be used to provide care to patients who are unable to leave their homes due to mobility issues or other health conditions.

Read more

Oxygen

Oxygen therapy is a medical treatment that involves supplying oxygen to patients who are experiencing respiratory distress or have low levels of oxygen in their blood. Oxygen therapy can be delivered through various devices, such as nasal cannulas or oxygen masks.
